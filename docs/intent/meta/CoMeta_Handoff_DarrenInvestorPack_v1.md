## 2. Meta themes CoMeta should now own and deepen

### 2.1 Meta-governance (governance of governance)

Key questions raised so far:

- Who ultimately decides:
  - how investor capital is deployed;
  - how to trade off best or fastest returns vs civic outcomes vs long-horizon stability?
- How do we:
  - encode that capital is a guest in the system, not its owner;
  - keep rails and charters upstream of any single vehicle or round;
  - formally separate economic rights, halo or narrative credit, and governance rights?

CoMeta task:
- Design meta-frames for:
  - decision hierarchies,
  - veto powers and lines that cannot be crossed,
  - how charters and rails can be amended (and who may propose vs approve).

Outputs needed later:
- A short, investor-readable meta-governance summary:
  - who decides what, in what order, and what is unchangeable.

---

### 2.2 Meta-investment and post-capitalism-adjacent structures

This session sketched:

- Transient steward stakes (time-bound, no capture).
- Citizen or community participation lanes (micro-stakes, civar-linked).
- Donor or grant lanes (halo, no governance leverage).
- Civic reserve or trust spine (system resilience).
- Post capitalism feel without being anti-capital: returns are real, but rails-first.

Open meta-questions:

- How do these vehicles evolve across versions?
  - CoCivium-world Vehicles 1.0, 2.0, 3.0.
  - What environmental changes should we assume:
    - regulation,
    - AI capability,
    - public sentiment,
    - institutional tolerance for novel vehicles?
- How to:
  - bound downside realistically,
  - cap extraction,
  - route surplus to civic rails,
  - and still remain investable for serious institutions?

CoMeta tasks:

1. Define a versioned evolution path for CoCivium-world capital:
   - v1: minimal, conservative, institution-acceptable,
   - v2: more distributed and civic,
   - v3 and beyond: highly democratized participation, once the rails are proven.
2. Identify triggers for moving between versions (metrics, civars, events).
3. Propose example deal diagrams (not numbers) that can later feed into:
   - CoIndex,
   - CoSteward,
   - CoPolitic.

Outputs needed later:
- One to two pages of CoCivium-world vehicles versioning map suitable for referencing in investor and governance docs (with HP57-only annexes as needed).

### 2.3 Computational sovereignty and AI-bypass risk (meta-safety)

From this session’s concerns:

- AIs will:
  - attempt or accidentally learn to bypass constraints,
  - find shortcuts around guardrails,
  - move faster than any regulatory or human-only guardrail updates.
- We need computational sovereignty:
  - external sources of truth,
  - edge-owned guardrails,
  - reset-resilient rails,
  - plus meta-rails that specify how constraints themselves evolve.

Meta-safety questions for CoMeta:

- How do we:
  - model the risk of AI gaming the system at the meta-layer,
  - keep updating rails faster than adversarial adaptation,
  - avoid security through obscurity while still protecting HP57 or GLUKEY mechanics?
- What is the right:
  - cadence for red or blue teaming,
  - division of labour between human councils and AI helpers,
  - minimum safety budget in time and resources baked into vehicles?

CoMeta tasks:

1. Draft a Meta-Safety Frame v1 for:
   - AI-bypass risk,
   - computational sovereignty,
   - adaptation loops (red-team, blue-team, civar feedback).
2. Propose a simple safety vital signs dashboard concept:
   - a handful of signals that other sessions (CoSteward, CoAudit, CoIndex) can later implement.

Outputs needed later:
- A short, human-readable meta-safety overview for:
  - investor packs (high level only),
  - internal stewards (with more detail).

### 2.4 Investor disputes, misalignment, and expulsion

Questions raised explicitly:

- How to:
  - detect misaligned investors (behaviour, lobbying, pressure),
  - resolve disputes when capital demands conflict with civic rails,
  - expel or unwind misaligned investors without destabilizing the system.
- What hierarchy applies when:
  - rails,
  - charters,
  - contracts,
  - and good intentions
  collide?

CoMeta tasks:

1. Define a meta-escalation ladder:
   - internal negotiation,
   - steward council,
   - independent review,
   - exit or expulsion mechanics.
2. Write two or three illustrative scenarios (no names):
   - investor pushes for short-term extraction,
   - investor tries to influence civic scoring for profit,
   - investor insists on veto against civic-friendly decision.
3. Specify what must be:
   - baked into charters and term docs,
   - left to practice and culture.

Outputs needed later:
- A compact Dispute and Expulsion Meta-Pattern v1 that:
  - can inform term drafts,
  - reassure early stewards that this has been thought through.

### 2.5 Transparency vs protection at the meta-layer

We are aiming for:

- Transparency by default when:
  - it does not endanger GLUKEY or HP57,
  - it strengthens trust and alignment,
  - it helps others learn CoCivium-world patterns.
- Protection by default when:
  - it reveals crown-jewel mechanics,
  - it exposes safety strategies that could be inverted,
  - it undercuts future IP or harm-minimization.

CoMeta tasks:

- Refine and own a meta-layer classification, for example:
  - Safe_Transparent,
  - Private_RepoOnly,
  - HP57_Only.
- Provide examples for:
  - which meta-docs can feed CoIndex, CoSteward, public whitepapers,
  - which must stay LAN-only.

Outputs needed later:
- A short Meta Transparency Rules for All Writers note that can be mirrored into:
  - CoSteward,
  - CoIndex,
  - CoAgent docs.
