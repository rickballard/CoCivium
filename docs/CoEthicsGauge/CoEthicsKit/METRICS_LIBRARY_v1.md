## Axis 1 — Democracy & Civic Power

### D1. Political Funding Alignment

- **Axis:** Democracy & Civic Power
- **Description:** Alignment of political donations (including PACs and similar vehicles) with democratic safeguards
  such as independent courts, electoral integrity, non-violent transfer of power, and rule of law.
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Major, recurring funding of actors or campaigns documented as undermining democratic safeguards.
  - -1: Noticeable skew in funding towards actors with poor democratic integrity records.
  -  0: Mixed or low-intensity spending; no strong pattern.
  - +1: Majority of funding supports actors that strengthen democratic safeguards.
  - +2: Clear, consistent pattern of funding aligned with pro-democracy norms and protections.
- **Evidence hints:**
  - official donation registries,
  - independent watchdog reports,
  - credible long-form journalism on political spending.

### D2. Lobbying and Regulatory Capture Risk

- **Axis:** Democracy & Civic Power
- **Description:** Whether lobbying efforts tend to weaken or strengthen public-interest regulation, transparency and accountability.
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Systematic lobbying for weaker oversight, reduced transparency, or concentration of unaccountable power.
  - -1: Frequent lobbying for narrower exemptions or loopholes that erode oversight.
  -  0: Mixed or unclear pattern; limited evidence.
  - +1: Occasional lobbying in support of transparency, safety, or accountability measures.
  - +2: Clear pattern of supporting regulations that defend public interest and democratic checks.
- **Evidence hints:**
  - lobbying registers and disclosures,
  - legislative histories,
  - watchdog analyses of specific bills.

## Axis 2 — Human Dignity & Labour

### L1. Core Labour Standards

- **Axis:** Human Dignity & Labour
- **Description:** Respect for core labour standards (no forced or child labour, non-discrimination, freedom of association).
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Repeated, credible reports of core labour rights violations without adequate remediation.
  - -1: Significant but less systemic issues, or slow/partial remediation.
  -  0: Limited information or mixed record.
  - +1: Clear policies and some independent verification of compliance.
  - +2: Strong policies, transparent audits, and a track record of proactive remediation.
- **Evidence hints:** ILO reports, NGO investigations, audits, court cases, worker testimony.

### L2. Workplace Health & Safety

- **Axis:** Human Dignity & Labour
- **Description:** Quality of health and safety conditions in workplaces directly controlled by the brand.
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Frequent serious accidents or fatalities, weak safety culture, little remediation.
  - -1: Noticeable safety incidents; some improvements but ongoing concerns.
  -  0: Average industry performance; incomplete data.
  - +1: Better-than-average safety metrics and transparency.
  - +2: Strong, verified safety culture and low incident rates.
- **Evidence hints:** regulator stats, safety audits, worker surveys.

## Axis 3 — Health & Wellbeing

### H1. Product Health Impact

- **Axis:** Health & Wellbeing
- **Description:** Net health impact of core products relative to public health guidance.
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Core portfolio dominated by high-risk products with minimal reformulation or mitigation.
  - -1: Significant share of high-risk products; some reformulation steps visible.
  -  0: Mixed portfolio with unclear net effect.
  - +1: Majority of products compatible with mainstream health guidelines.
  - +2: Brand identity centred on products that are clearly health-supporting.
- **Evidence hints:** nutrient profiles, epidemiological studies where applicable, official guidance.

### H2. Marketing to Vulnerable Groups

- **Axis:** Health & Wellbeing
- **Description:** Degree to which marketing targets children or other vulnerable groups with higher-risk offerings.
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Persistent marketing of high-risk offerings to vulnerable groups despite criticism or guidance.
  - -1: Some problematic campaigns; partial restrictions.
  -  0: Little data or mixed signals.
  - +1: Policies limiting such marketing, mostly adhered to.
  - +2: Strong, enforced commitments not to target vulnerable groups with high-risk products.
- **Evidence hints:** advertising audits, self-regulatory bodies, campaign archives.

## Axis 4 — Planet & Future Generations

### E1. Emissions Trajectory vs Public Targets

- **Axis:** Planet & Future Generations
- **Description:** How the company’s greenhouse-gas emissions trajectory compares with its stated targets and with recognised pathways.
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Emissions rising or flat; targets weak or absent.
  - -1: Modest improvements that clearly lag credible pathways.
  -  0: Targets exist; limited evidence of decisive movement.
  - +1: Emissions trending down in line with stated targets.
  - +2: Emissions reductions consistent with ambitious pathways and verified progress.
- **Evidence hints:** sustainability reports, third-party verifications, independent analyses.

## Axis 5 — Data, Information & AI

### A1. Data Exploitation & Consent

- **Axis:** Data, Information & AI
- **Description:** Respect for user privacy, clarity of consent, and limits on secondary use of data.
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Repeated misuse of data, dark patterns, or large-scale privacy violations.
  - -1: Noticeable issues or opaque practices; partial remediation.
  -  0: Standard industry practices; not clearly better or worse.
  - +1: Clear consent flows, minimised data collection, transparent policies.
  - +2: Strong privacy design, independent oversight, and privacy-by-default patterns.
- **Evidence hints:** regulator cases, privacy policies, independent audits.

## Axis 6 — Integrity, Fairness & Governance

### G1. Tax Justice & Profit Shifting

- **Axis:** Integrity, Fairness & Governance
- **Description:** Whether tax practices align with the spirit as well as the letter of the law in major jurisdictions.
- **Scale (-2..+2):**
  - -2: Aggressive profit shifting and secrecy jurisdictions as core strategy.
  - -1: Notable use of tax havens; limited transparency.
  -  0: Limited information; appears roughly in line with peers.
  - +1: Public commitments to responsible tax; some transparency.
  - +2: High transparency, clear policy against aggressive avoidance, and independent recognition.
- **Evidence hints:** tax transparency reports, NGO analyses, corporate disclosures.
